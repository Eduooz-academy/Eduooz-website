Eligibility & Age Requirements.

Eligibility Criteria

Educational Qualification

Candidates typically must have 10+2 Science with a Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology (DMLT), B.Sc Medical Laboratory Technology, or another recognized qualification specified for the respective MLT post. The exact qualification can vary depending on the participating AIIMS institution and post code.

Registration

Valid registration with a recognized Paramedical Council or competent authority may be required, where applicable. Educational certificates, registration documents, and other supporting records must be produced during document verification.

Experience

Relevant laboratory experience may be required or preferred depending on the specific AIIMS CRE MLT post. Candidates should have practical knowledge of laboratory procedures, diagnostic techniques, equipment handling, and sample processing.

Age Limit

The age limit is determined by the official AIIMS CRE notification and specific post requirements. Age relaxation applies to eligible reserved categories as per Government of India rules.

Nationality

Candidates must generally be Indian citizens and satisfy the nationality conditions prescribed under AIIMS recruitment rules.

Medical Fitness

Selected candidates must be medically and physically fit to perform Medical Laboratory Technologist duties. A medical examination may form part of the recruitment process.

Everything you need to know.

From exam eligibility to career growth, find answers to your most pressing CRE MLT Exam preparation questions.

The CRE MLT Exam (Common Recruitment Examination – Medical Laboratory Technician) is a competitive government exam conducted to select eligible candidates for Medical Laboratory Technician (MLT) positions in hospitals, diagnostic labs, and healthcare institutions under central or state government departments.

Candidates must have a Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology (DMLT), B.Sc Medical Laboratory Technology (B.Sc MLT), or other equivalent recognized laboratory science qualifications.

Candidates generally require a Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology (DMLT) from a recognized institution, a B.Sc in Medical Laboratory Technology (B.Sc MLT), or a paramedical certificate or degree approved by government health authorities.

Yes, candidates may need registration with the State Paramedical or Health Council, depending on the government rules mentioned in the notification.

The typical age limit is 18–35 years, with relaxation for SC, ST, OBC, EWS, and PwBD candidates as per government norms.

The selection process generally includes a Written Examination (Objective/MCQ format), a Practical Laboratory Skill Test, Document Verification, and an Interview or Viva-Voce (if applicable).

The exam pattern includes multiple-choice questions (MCQs) covering Medical Laboratory Technology subjects, general knowledge and reasoning, and basic computer skills for lab data management.

A CRE MLT performs laboratory tests, prepares samples and reagents, maintains laboratory equipment, records data accurately, and supports senior technicians in diagnostic analysis.

Applicants generally need educational certificates, identity proof and age proof, a registration certificate (if required), an experience certificate (if applicable), and a passport-size photograph and signature.

Career growth opportunities include Senior Laboratory Technician, Laboratory Supervisor, Technical Officer, Quality Control Officer, and Laboratory Manager.
