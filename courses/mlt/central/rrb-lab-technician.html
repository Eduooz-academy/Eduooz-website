Who Can Apply & Age Criteria

Eligibility & Age Requirements.

Eligibility Criteria

Educational Qualification

Candidates typically must have 12th (10+2) with Science subjects and a Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology (DMLT) or an equivalent recognized MLT qualification. Candidates with B.Sc Medical Laboratory Technology (B.Sc MLT) may also be eligible depending on the exact RRB post and recruitment notification. The qualification must be obtained from a recognized institution.

Registration Requirement

Valid registration with a recognized Paramedical Council or competent authority may be required where applicable. Candidates must produce educational certificates, registration records, and other required documents during verification.

Clinical / Professional Experience

Previous laboratory experience may be preferred or required depending on the specific recruitment notification. Candidates should have practical knowledge of laboratory procedures, sample handling, diagnostic techniques, and laboratory safety practices.

Age Limit and Relaxation

The age limit is determined by the official RRB Paramedical recruitment notification and the specific post. Age relaxation applies to eligible reserved categories according to Government of India and railway recruitment rules.

Nationality Requirement

Candidates must generally be Indian citizens and satisfy the eligibility conditions specified by the Railway Recruitment Board.

Medical Fitness

Candidates must meet the prescribed Railway medical fitness standards for the relevant laboratory post. A medical examination is conducted as part of the recruitment process.

Everything you need to know.

From exam eligibility to career growth, find answers to your most pressing RRB MLT Exam preparation questions.

The RRB MLT Exam is a competitive examination conducted by the Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) to recruit Medical Laboratory Technicians (MLT) for various Indian Railways hospitals, health units, and diagnostic laboratories.

Candidates must have a Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology (DMLT), B.Sc Medical Laboratory Technology (B.Sc MLT), or equivalent recognized laboratory technology qualifications.

Candidates generally require DMLT from a recognized institution, B.Sc in Medical Laboratory Technology (B.Sc MLT), or a paramedical diploma or certificate approved by government authorities.

The selection process generally includes a Computer-Based Test (CBT) or written examination, document verification, a skill or practical test (if applicable), and merit list preparation.

The exam pattern usually includes objective-type multiple-choice questions (MCQs) covering clinical biochemistry, hematology, microbiology, pathology, laboratory techniques and instrumentation, and general knowledge and reasoning.

An RRB Medical Laboratory Technician conducts diagnostic laboratory tests, prepares and analyzes clinical samples, maintains lab equipment, records and reports test results, and supports senior laboratory staff in hospital and diagnostic procedures.

Applicants generally need educational certificates (DMLT/B.Sc MLT), identity proof and age proof, a registration certificate (if required), an experience certificate (if applicable), and a passport-size photograph and signature.

Benefits include stable government employment under Indian Railways, regular salary with allowances, career growth in clinical and diagnostic laboratories, and exposure to advanced laboratory equipment and techniques.

Career growth opportunities include Senior Laboratory Technician, Laboratory Supervisor, Technical Officer, Quality Control Officer, and Laboratory Manager.

Key skills include clinical laboratory testing, sample collection and handling, accuracy and attention to detail, laboratory data recording and reporting, and knowledge of lab safety protocols.
