Who Can Apply & Age Criteria

Eligibility & Age Requirements.

Eligibility Criteria

Educational Qualification

Candidates typically must possess a Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology (DMLT) or B.Sc Medical Laboratory Technology (B.Sc MLT) from a recognized institution. The qualification must satisfy the requirements prescribed by Kerala PSC and the relevant Homoeo / Indian Systems of Medicine recruitment rules.

Registration Requirement

Valid registration with the Kerala Paramedical Council or another recognized competent authority may be required depending on the official notification. Where professional registration is mandatory, it must remain valid at the relevant stages of application and appointment.

Clinical / Professional Experience

Relevant laboratory experience in hospitals, laboratories, diagnostic centers, or healthcare institutions may be preferred or required for specific posts. Candidates should have practical knowledge of laboratory procedures, specimen handling, diagnostic techniques, equipment operation, and laboratory safety practices.

Age Limit and Relaxation Details

The age limit is prescribed in the specific Kerala PSC IMS Homoeo MLT recruitment notification. Eligible reserved categories receive age relaxation according to applicable Kerala Government and Kerala PSC rules.

Nationality Requirement

Candidates must satisfy the nationality and eligibility conditions specified by Kerala PSC. Applicable citizenship requirements must be met according to the recruitment rules.

Medical Fitness

Candidates must be medically and physically fit to perform the duties of a Medical Laboratory Technician. Medical fitness verification may be required during the appointment process under the applicable government service rules.

Complete Curriculum

Official Exam Syllabus.

Module 1: Hematology (5 Marks)
  • Composition of blood, anticoagulants, blood cell count, hematopoiesis
  • Blood cell morphology (normal & abnormal), functions of blood
  • Thin & thick smear preparation, staining of blood films, common stains
  • Demonstration of blood cells, bone marrow smear, sickle cell & LE cell preparation
  • Leukemia, demonstration of blood parasites
  • Hemoglobin: functions, normal & abnormal hemoglobin, estimation methods, anemia
  • PCV, ESR, blood cell indices, osmotic fragility
  • Blood coagulation & hemostasis, disorders of coagulation, coagulation tests, factor analysis
Module 2: Clinical Pathology – Basic Techniques (5 Marks)
  • Urine analysis: physical, chemical, microscopy
  • Examination of faeces, sputum, cerebrospinal fluid, other body fluids
  • Semen analysis
Module 3: Histotechnology (5 Marks)
  • Introduction to histology, design of a histology lab, specimen reception & recording
  • Types of tissues & characteristics influencing processing
  • Fixatives: purpose, ideal & common fixatives, removal of formalin pigment, decalcification
  • Microtome: principle, maintenance, types, knives, sharpening & care
  • Paraffin section preparation: tissue processing, embedding, automatic tissue processors, fault correction, block storage
  • Staining & mounting: principles, common & special stains, immunohistochemistry basics, section storage
  • Frozen sections: freezing microtomes, cryostat, preparation, staining, advantages
Module 4: Cytology (5 Marks)
  • Applications of cytology, specimen collection (gynaecological & non-gynaecological)
  • Smear preparation, fixation, Papanicolaou staining, Shorr's stain, other stains
  • Buccal smear – Barr body demonstration
  • Fine needle aspiration cytology (FNAC)
  • Concentration techniques: centrifugation, cyto-centrifugation, membrane filters, cell blocks
  • Liquid-based cytology – monolayer preparation
Module 5: Cytogenetics (5 Marks)
  • Karyotyping – methods of chromosome analysis with blood & bone marrow
  • Banding techniques, general aspects of chromosomes, chromosome defects
  • Advanced methods in cytogenetics
Module 6: Blood Banking (5 Marks)
  • Theoretical aspects of blood grouping & banking, blood cell antigens, mode of inheritance, antigen-antibody concept
  • ABO & Rh blood group systems, other systems, grouping & typing techniques
  • Direct & indirect Coombs' test, compatibility testing
  • Donor selection & screening, collection & storage of blood, anticoagulants
  • Preparation & storage of blood components
  • Transfusion reactions, transfusion-transmitted diseases
  • Apheresis, plasmapheresis, automation & recent developments in blood banking
Module 1: General Microbiology (6 Marks)
  • History & scope of microbiology, laboratory safety & personal protection
  • Sterilization & disinfection methods & applications, biomedical waste management
  • Guidelines on specimen reception, recording, handling, cultures & lab waste
  • Microscopes: compound, darkfield, phase contrast, fluorescent & electron microscopy
Module 2: Bacteriology (6 Marks)
  • Morphology of bacteria, growth requirements, culture media, pH adjustment, sterilization & storage of media
  • Staining: simple, differential, special – principle, methods & application
  • Identification schemes, medically important bacteria, role in infection, lab diagnosis
  • Antimicrobial susceptibility testing: discs, methods, use in identification
  • Clinical bacteriology: sample collection, containers, transport media & preservatives
  • Processing of specimens: microscopy, culture, biochemical & serological identification, pathogenicity & antibiotic susceptibility tests
  • Serological & rapid techniques for bacterial infection diagnosis
Module 3: Parasitology (6 Marks)
  • Morphology & classification of protozoa & helminths, common diseases, life cycle, lab diagnosis
  • Clinical parasitology: specimen collection, transportation & storage
  • Processing: thin & thick blood smears, microfilaria, stool examination, concentration & staining methods
  • Serological diagnosis & other rapid tests
Module 4: Mycology (6 Marks)
  • Classification of fungi, dimorphic & common contaminant fungi, culture characteristics & identification
  • Common fungal diseases: cutaneous, sub-cutaneous/mucous, systemic & opportunistic
  • Clinical mycology: specimen collection & transportation
  • Processing: microscopy, culture media & techniques
Module 5: Virology (6 Marks)
  • Structure, classification (DNA & RNA viruses), cultivation & identification
  • Medically important viral diseases
  • Clinical virology: collection, transportation, storage & special handling of specimens
  • Processing: staining, fluorescent antibody techniques, viral isolation, inclusion bodies
  • Serological techniques in viral infection diagnosis
Module 1: Glassware & Volumetric Apparatus (2 Marks)
  • Composition of glass, varieties & grades of glassware, cleaning of glassware
  • Calibration of pipettes & other volumetric apparatus
Module 2: Laboratory Safety (1 Mark)
  • Laboratory hazards & safety, universal safety precautions, first aid in the laboratory
Module 3: Chemicals & Storage (1 Mark)
  • Grades of chemicals, secondary standard chemicals, storage of chemicals
Module 4: Water Purification (1 Mark)
  • Preparation & storage of distilled water, deionized water, pyrogen-free distilled water
  • Evaluation of water purity
Module 5: Units of Measurement (1 Mark)
  • SI units and non-SI units
Module 6: Solutions (1 Mark)
  • Preparation of normal, molar & percentage solutions
  • Dilution of solutions, interconversion of concentration (normal, molar, molal, percentage)
Module 7: Specimen Handling (3 Marks)
  • Collection, preparation, transportation, processing & disposal of biological specimens
  • Preparation & use of anticoagulants
  • Urine preservation
Module 8: Laboratory Instruments (1 Mark)
  • Principle, use & maintenance of colorimeter, spectrophotometer, fluorimeter, nephelometer, flame photometer
  • Atomic absorption spectrophotometer, luminometers, centrifuges, magnetic stirrer, vortex mixer, pH meter
Module 9: Radioisotopes (1 Mark)
  • Basic principles of radioactivity, detection & measurement, applications of radioactivity
Module 10: Buffers & Acid-Base Balance (2 Marks)
  • Buffer solutions, Henderson-Hasselbalch equation, pH indicators
  • Body buffer system, respiratory & renal disturbances, anion gap
  • Metabolic & respiratory acidosis/alkalosis, fluid & electrolyte balance, osmolality
  • Methodology of measuring blood pH, PCO2 & PO2
  • Physical chemistry: osmosis, dialysis, Donnan membrane equilibrium, viscosity, surface tension, colloids, emulsion, adsorption, partition coefficient
Module 11: Digestion & Absorption (1 Mark)
  • Digestion & absorption of carbohydrates, proteins & lipids
Module 12: Biomolecules & Metabolism (3 Marks)
  • Chemistry of carbohydrates, proteins, lipids & nucleic acids
  • Metabolism of carbohydrates, proteins, lipids, nucleic acids & minerals; inborn errors of metabolism
  • Vitamins & coenzymes, estimation of vitamins
  • Nutrition: nutritional importance & calorific values of food
  • Hemoglobin: function, synthesis & degradation of heme, derivatives, variants, jaundice, congenital disorders
  • Porphyria, disorders of porphyrin metabolism, lab diagnosis
Module 13: Enzymology (3 Marks)
  • Enzymes: classification, coenzymes, cofactors, mechanism of action, kinetics, MM constant, inhibition, regulatory enzymes
  • Clinical enzymology: activity determination (end point & kinetic assay) for ACP, ALP, AST, ALT, amylase, lipase, LDH, CK, G6PD, 5' nucleotidase, GGT, cholinesterase
  • Isoenzymes & diagnostic importance in MI, liver & muscular disease
Module 14: Hormones (1 Mark)
  • General properties & functions of hormones, mechanism of hormone action
  • Hypothalamic, pituitary, pancreatic, thyroid, parathyroid, adrenal cortex/medulla & gonadal hormones
  • Methods of estimation of hormones & hormone metabolites
Module 15: Molecular Biology (3 Marks)
  • DNA replication & repair, transcription & genetic code, translation, post-translational modification, protein folding & targeting
  • Molecular biology techniques: recombinant DNA technology, blotting techniques, PCR, FISH
  • Cloning, genomic library
Module 16: Urine & Body Fluid Analysis (2 Marks)
  • Composition of normal urine, abnormal constituents – qualitative & quantitative tests
  • Urinary screening for metabolic inherited disorders
  • Estimation of VMA, 17-ketosteroid, 5-HIAA, oestriol from urine
  • Clearance tests: creatinine, urea & inulin
  • CSF & other body fluids: composition & chemical analysis of CSF, synovial, peritoneal, pericardial & amniotic fluid
Module 17: Gastric Analysis (1 Mark)
  • Stomach tubes, fractional test meal
  • Chemical examination for bile pigments, blood, starch & gastric juice
  • Estimation of free & total acid, tubeless gastric function test
Module 18: Calculi Analysis (1 Mark)
  • Analysis of urinary & biliary calculi
Module 19: Electrophoresis (1 Mark)
  • Different methods, advantages & disadvantages
  • Electrophoresis of serum protein, hemoglobin & lipoproteins, and interpretation
Module 20: Chromatography (2 Marks)
  • Forms of chromatographic technique, applications, advantages & disadvantages
  • Urine aminogram, chromatographic separation of amino acids, sugar, lipids, drugs & toxins
Module 21: Immunology (2 Marks)
  • Infection, immunity, antigens, antibodies, immunoglobulins, complement system
  • Structure & function of immune system, immune response, immune deficiency diseases
  • Hypersensitivity, autoimmunity, immunology of transplantation & malignancies
  • Immunological methods: antigen-antibody reactions, immunodiffusion, immunoelectrophoresis, ELISA, fluorescent immunoassay, radioimmunoassay
Module 22: Clinical Biochemistry Laboratory Tests (1 Mark)
  • Liver, kidney, gastrointestinal, pancreatic, thyroid, gonadal & foeto-placental function tests
  • Malabsorption studies, lipid profile, cardiac markers, tumor markers, GTT, GCT, glycated hemoglobin
Module 23: Toxicology (1 Mark)
  • General methods of analysis & screening tests for common drugs used in therapy
Module 24: Laboratory Organization & Management (1 Mark)
  • Organization & management of a clinical biochemistry laboratory
Module 25: Automation & Informatics (2 Marks)
  • Automation: semi-auto & auto analyzers – types, advantages, recent trends
  • Laboratory informatics
Module 26: Quality Control (1 Mark)
  • Accuracy, precision, sensitivity, specificity, standard deviation
  • Preanalytical & analytical variables, QC charts, QC sera
  • Internal & external quality control programme

Everything you need to know.

From eligibility specifics to the selection process, find the answers to your most pressing questions about the IMS Homeo MLT Exam.

The Kerala PSC IMS Homeo MLT Exam is a state-level recruitment examination conducted by the Kerala Public Service Commission for the post of Medical Laboratory Technician (MLT) under the Indian Systems of Medicine (IMS) Homoeo Department, Kerala.

Candidates typically require a Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology (DMLT) or B.Sc Medical Laboratory Technology (B.Sc MLT) from a recognized institution, as prescribed in the official Kerala PSC recruitment notification.

Depending on the official notification, candidates may need valid registration with the Kerala Paramedical Council or another recognized competent authority, which must remain valid at the relevant stages of application and appointment.

The age limit is prescribed in the specific Kerala PSC IMS Homoeo MLT recruitment notification. Eligible reserved categories receive age relaxation according to applicable Kerala Government and Kerala PSC rules.

The selection process generally includes a written examination, document verification, medical fitness verification, and final appointment, based on the rank list prepared as per Kerala PSC rules.

The exam pattern generally includes objective-type multiple-choice questions covering Medical Laboratory Technology subjects, General Awareness, and Aptitude, as specified in the official Kerala PSC notification.

A Medical Laboratory Technician in a Homoeo dispensary or hospital performs diagnostic tests, collects and handles specimens, operates laboratory equipment, maintains records, and follows laboratory safety protocols to support clinical diagnosis and patient care.

Applicants typically need educational certificates, professional registration certificate (where applicable), identity proof, category certificate (if applicable), passport-size photograph, signature, and other documents required as per the official notification.

Candidates can apply online through the official Kerala PSC Thulasi portal by completing registration, filling out the application form, uploading documents, and submitting the application before the closing date stated in the notification.

Selected Lab Technicians can progress to positions such as Senior Lab Technician, Lab Supervisor, and Quality Control Officer, along with other promotional opportunities within the Kerala Government healthcare service.
